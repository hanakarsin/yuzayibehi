KMJA Sues DCI, DPP Over Arrest of Justices Said Chitembwe and Aggrey Muchelule

May 2024 · 5 minute read
  • Prosecutor Danstan Omari claimed that officers from the DCI wanted to plant money in the judges' chambers then accuse them of taking bribes
  • Omari also said that the State has been arresting members of the judiciary with the intent of stripping them of their dignity
  • KMJA further petitioned the courts seeking orders barring the DPP and DCI from being arrested over KSh 5 million bribery allegations

Kenya Magistrates and Judges Association have condemned the arrest of Justice Aggrey Muchelule and Said Chitembwe, saying it was an orchestrated attack by the state meant to embarrass them.

DPP, DCI stopped from persecuting Muchelule, Chitembwe

Through their lawyers Danstan Omari, Cliff Ombeta and Shadrack Wambui, KMJA sued the Director of Public Prosecution (DPP) and Directorate of Criminal Investigation, seeking orders to stop their arrest and prosecution.

While addressing the press at the Milimani Law Courts after filing the case, Omari claimed officers from the DCI wanted to plant money in the judges' chambers then accuse them of taking bribes.

Omari said that the State has been arresting members of the judiciary with the intent of stripping them of their dignity.

"The state, having worn to revisit the Judiciary, is an affront to the independence of the judiciary. The actions of the judges were meant to humiliate the judges and strip them of their dignity."

Bribery allegations

KMJA have petitioned the courts seeking orders barring the DPP and DCI from being arrested over KSh 5 million bribery allegations.

In a certificate of urgency filed before Milimani law courts, KJMA, through lawyer Dunstan Omari wants the court to issue orders resting the state from further arresting or charging the two Judges in any court within the republic of Kenya.

"That the court is pleased to issue a conservatory order forthwith restraining the DPP and DCI their servants, agents or assistants from arresting, harassing, arraigning or charging the justices Muchelule and Chitembwe pending hearing and determination of the application," KJMA said in the court documents.

The lawyer also wants the court to issue an order barring the two judges from taking pleas in any criminal court within the jurisdictions until the petition is heard and determined.

"That without doubt following the arrest, search and being required to record a statement, the prospective charges against the judges are likely to be commenced in abuse of the court process with the singular objective of embarrassing them and are otherwise oppressive, vexatious and harassing, "Omari said.

He further claimed that following their arrest, search and requirement to record statements over baseless prospective charges are likely to be levelled against the two charges; it is clear that the impugned criminal proceedings have been commenced with an ulterior motive are malicious.

"The court moves with speed and stops the arraignment and or formal charging of the applicants in the basis of the DCI spurious allegations, then the Independence of the Judiciary will henceforth be threatened with encroachment by the DPP and the DCI, " Omari argues.

Omari added that no prejudice will be occasioned to any person if a stay order are granted, barring the state from prosecuting the two Judges.

He noted that the petition by the judges raises serious and fundamental constitutional issues that may affect the liberty and dignity of the two judges and has a high chance of success.

According to court papers, the two Judges said that the DCI officers searched their offices on Thursday, July 22, without having a search warrant.

And still, nothing was found at their offices to link them to the bribery allegations facing them.

The move comes after the two judges were arrested at their chamber or office at Mililani law court by detectives from DCI headquarters and taken to Kiambu road where they were reportedly grilled over bribery claims.

They, however, both denied knowledge of the allegations adding that their offices were searched, but nothing was found.

“I am equally surprised like you are. They also questioned my clerk," Muchelule said.

Detectives from the DCI's Serious Crimes Unit (SCU) led by the head Obadiah Kuria had obtained a search warrant from a magistrate court.

Muchelule, who was among the six judges President Uhuru Kenyatta rejected their appointment citing integrity issues, is currently attached to Milimani Family Division.

Justice Chitembwe was in April this year unsuccessfully interviewed for the position of Chief Justice to succeed David Maraga.

He is currently serving in the Civil Division of the High Court at Milimani. Chitembwe had been arrested in December 2009 by the anti-graft agency together with the former NSSF managing trustee, Rachel Lumbasyo. He was later acquitted.

The two were accompanied by their clerks and support staff, who were later released, reportedly, unconditionally.

Before the judges were taken to the DCI headquarters, officers conducted thorough searches in their offices.
