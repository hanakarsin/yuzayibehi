Analysis: Possibility of ODM, One Kenya Alliance Coalition Should Worry William Ruto

April 2024 · 4 minute read

- This week’s meeting involving Raila Odinga, Muhoho and Gideon Moi has been billed as highly significant

- Notably, it came barely two days after President Uhuru and Raila Odinga held a six-hour meeting

-According to analysts, the meeting was a clear confirmation that the handshake remains intact

- More crucially, it could be a pointer to a scheme by Uhuru and Raila to form a formidable coalition with One Kenya Alliance

- The get-together also effectively poured cold water on any talk of a looming Raila-Ruto alliance

A flurry of meetings involving prominent politicians has been the talk of the town in recent days, generating all manner of speculation about possible alliances ahead of the 2022 General Election.

While the meeting between Deputy President William Ruto and Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya sent tongues wagging, it is a meeting involving members of three of Kenya’s most influential families this week that has thrown politicians and political pundits alike in a spin.

The meeting, which brought together ODM leader Raila Odinga, President Uhuru Kenyatta’s younger brother Muhoho and KANU chairman Gideon Moi in Raila’s Karen home on Tuesday, April 13, is seen to be as significant as it was surprising.

Notably, it came barely two days after Uhuru and Raila held a six-hour meeting.

According to some analysts, Tuesday’s meeting was a clear confirmation that the handshake remains intact and that Uhuru is keen to have the opposition leader as a core part of his 2022 succession matrix.

This get-together also effectively poured cold water on any talk of a looming Raila-Ruto political alliance, with Ruto having recently launched a full-blown charm offensive in wooing Baba to his side.

Even more crucially, there is a feeling that Tuesday’s meeting could be a pointer to a scheme to have Uhuru and Raila form a bigger coalition that also includes the nascent yet formidable One Kenya Alliance, which has Moi, Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka, ANC boss Musalia Mudavadi and Ford Kenya's Moses Wetang’ula as its principals.

According to Herman Manyora, a political analyst, there is a likelihood that One Kenya Alliance will eventually be convinced to support Raila in next year’s polls, making it a duel between Raila and Ruto.

“The One Kenya Alliance will be convinced to support Raila in 2022, it is going to be a Raila against Ruto contest,” the University of Nairobi lecturer said.

Such a setting will drastically shrink Ruto’s chances of winning the presidency.

Manyora adds that the meeting also put to rest fears that the president’s fixers are plotting to pick his successor from the One Kenya Alliance at Raila’s expense.

“It was a mission to allay fears and show Raila’s supporters that nothing has gone wrong, Uhuru really wants to work with Raila ahead of 2022,” he noted.

His argument on the 2022 partnership is echoed by political analyst Dismas Mokua, who says the Raila-Muhoho-Moi meeting proved that One Kenya Alliance could be a workable proposition, with key players determined to buy shares early in the day.

Mokua averred that Uhuru and Raila are joined at the hip as far as the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) is concerned and have no option but to work together at least until the referendum is held.

Nyeri Town MP Ngunjiri Wambugu said Tuesday’s meeting epitomised political strength as the push to amend the constitution through the BBI process hits the homestretch.

“This shows that no alliances will work if the BBI is not at the centre. The meeting means that there is a sense of first having the BBI sail through before talking about 2022 coalitions,” the vocal legislator stated.

On Tuesday night, two key leaders allied to Raila and Ruto dismissed claims that there were any plans whatsoever by the duo to form an alliance ahead of 2022.

Kipchumba Murkomen, an ally of the DP, said they had not and were not planning to have a conversation towards forming a political alliance with ODM and that they only saw Raila as their only formidable opponent.

On his part, ODM chairman John Mbadi said they had not held any talks with Ruto and that ODM’s focus is solely on BBI.

They spoke during an interview on Citizen Television.
