Uhuru Kenyatta Says He Has Done Better in His Second Term than First Term

President Uhuru Kenyatta has said that he has done better in his second team in terms of development than in the first term of presidency.

President Uhuru delivered more projects

According to the president, he focused more on development in his second term than in the first term, where the political agenda dominated.

Speaking on Monday, June 14, when he hosted a delegation of political leaders from Ukambani in State House Nairobi, Uhuru pointed out that working with leaders from across the political divide enabled him to deliver more projects during his second term in office.

“Working in conjunction with my colleagues, I have been able to focus on the development agenda, not the political agenda,” the president said.

Political leaders to work towards uniting Kenyans

At the same time, Uhuru urged leaders across the political divide to unite and work together for Kenya to become a middle-income country by 2030.

The head of state also maintained that the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) was to bring the country together and consolidate national unity, not for creating positions as many claimed.

He also cautioned leaders against endless politics at the expense of developing, saying political bickering and selfish pursuits by some leaders were holding the country back.

Former vice president Kalonzo Musyoka, who led the delegation, lauded Uhuru for the development projects implemented by the national government in the Ukambani region, citing the Kenya Meat Commission and several other projects.

This meeting with leaders from Ukambani comes a few weeks after the president hosted elected Nyanza leaders on Friday, May 28, before Madaraka Day celebrations in Kisumu.

Uhuru said he and ODM party leader Raila Odinga were determined to leave a united country.

He noted that divisive politics had derailed the country's development agenda for far too long.
