Gospel Singer Ben Cyco Marks Late Sister Joy's Death Anniversary with Emotional Message

May 2024 · 3 minute read

- Ben Cyco's sister Joy passed away in 2020 after battling leukaemia for years

- The gospel singer paid tribute to her while visiting her grave in Murang'a

- He also sent a message to those who are in mourning, saying grieving was a painful experience

Gospel singer Ben Cyco has paid tribute to his sister, who passed away from cancer.

On the anniversary of Joy Wairuri's death, the gospel singer visited her grave in Murang’a county and sent a heartfelt message.

In the photo, the downcast singer is seen staring at a picture of the deceased.

Ben said he could not believe a year had already gone by. He also sent a message of encouragement to those dealing with grief, saying it is not easy.

“Continue resting @_fu.raha_. Can't believe it's already one year.

Before her sudden demise, Joy had been battling leukaemia for a couple of years.

Announcing her untimely death, Ben expressed sorrow while giving glory to God for taking her to a place where there is no suffering.

"Rest well, small sis, you fought cancer with all you had. You fought a good fight, and now we give glory to God because where you are, there's no more suffering. I thank God for a life well-lived. I'm in tears writing this, but it is well. Rest in peace, Joy Wairuri," he wrote.

According to Ben, the last encounter they had was when he set up a Netflix account for her not to get bored.

“Though I'm sad, I'm at peace with the fact that your faith in God was unshaken, and now you are in Heaven. You are in a better place, Joy," he added.
