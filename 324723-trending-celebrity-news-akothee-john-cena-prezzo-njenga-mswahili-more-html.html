2. Eric Omondi discloses how comedian Njenga Mswahili suffered before his death

Big Tyme Entertainment CEO Eric Omondi has let the cat out of the bag about the troubles Anthony Njenga, better known as Njenga Mswahili went through before his demise.

Speaking in an interview with a local radio station, Eric revealed that the late comedian was never okay for the last three years. He stated that Njenga would seek help from his friends and other people.

Omondi cited that he one day even did some shopping for him. However, he didn’t bother to know what Njenga was going through. For this reason, he partly blames himself for assuming that the late comedian would manage to navigate through all his problems alone.

According to Omondi, celebrities, especially comedians struggle to maintain a particular image in front of their fans and social media opting to suffer in silence.

Njenga Mswahili was not okay for nearly three years. He sought help from different people, including myself. He approached me on two or three occasions for assistance. I helped where I could. The last time I bumped into him, he told me that he had gotten saved, and was preaching at Junction area in Dagoretti. I asked him to get into the supermarket and pick the things that he needed, which he did. I settled the bill, revealed Omondi.

A few months later, Eric got to know that Njenga Mswahili was being arrested on theft grounds. At that instance, he knew that Njenga was undeniably battling with a lot of depression.

I was shocked, given I knew he was born again. Much later, I learned that he was suffering depression. This is something that very many people knew, but today, life is akin to how people get fast-food meals. Somebody could be shouting for help, but no one cares to help. Talking about how celebrities struggle to maintain a particular public image, Big Tyme Entertainment CEO referenced the instances when he was very famous and had made it on the TV but was using public transport as he couldn’t afford to buy a car.

When you are a celebrity who has a big name with no money, then chances are high you would suffer depression, given the lifestyle you’re living is not mirroring your status as a public figure, said Omondi.

7. Reports reveal that Nyashinski changed his name before his traditional wedding

Recent reports in the entertainment news world indicate that rapper Nyashinski changed his official title. Through a gazette notice issued on November 1st, he dropped his first name, Inyatta.

The singer, whose real name is Inyatta Nyamari Ongegu, now wants to be called Nyamari Ongegu.

Notice is given that by a deed poll dated the 10th April 2019 duly executed and registered in the registry of Documents at Nairobi. Formerly known as Inyatta Nyamari Ongegu formally and absolutely renounced and abandoned the use of his former name Inyatta Nyamari Ongegu, and in lieu thereof assumed and adopted the name Nyamari Ongegu, for all purposes and authorizes and requests all persons at all times to designate, describe and address him by his assumed name Nyamari Ongegu only, a part of the gazette notice reads.

Even though the Mungu Pekee singer has remained silent on the matter, it is now clear that the decision was arrived at before his traditional wedding with Kalenjin lover Zippy Jepkemei Bett.
