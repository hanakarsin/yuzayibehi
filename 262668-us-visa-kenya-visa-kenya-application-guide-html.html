US Visa Kenya: Detailed US Visa in Kenya application guide

June 2024 · 10 minute read

The process of acquiring a US Visa while in Kenya is no longer as difficult and stressful as it was in the past days. Presently, the United States government made several amendments to make it easier for Kenyans who want to visit or travel to the United States. To do so, individuals must undertake defined procedures and processes which are simple and straightforward. This article will provide a detailed guide on how one can conveniently apply for a US Visa Kenya.

How to Get a US Visa Kenya

There is one key travel document that one requires before they can apply for a United States visa. This includes an immigration passport that can be obtained at your nearest immigration office depending on your location. After getting your immigration passport, you can now start processing.

Types of US Visa

Any Kenyan citizen that would like to travel to the United States of America must acquire a traveling card which is categorized into two main types that are Immigrant and non-immigrant. Immigrant cards are given to persons from other nations that intend to live permanently in the United States while non-immigrant United States visas are meant for those individuals that wish to visit the United States temporarily. There are several forms of Non-immigrant permits depending on the purpose of visit as outlined below;

  • Work Visa: It is issued to any Kenyan who has received employment from a United States proprietor. It allows the bearer to live and work in the country for a specified period.
  • Student Visa: It is given to transnational scholars who wish to study full-time at any recognized academic institution based in the United States. It allows the student to live in the country during the period of their study.
  • Family Visa: It is issued to United States Citizens that would like to act as guarantors for their families that include children, spouses, wives, or parents to join them in the United States.
  • Tourist/Business Visa: It is also is granted to individuals who have to travel temporarily for business reasons (B1) or tourist visitation (B2).

Before applying for a US visa, one has to have the following requirements

  • An immigration passport that must be valid six months after the intended stay.
  • A passport picture with specific size requirements (2’’by2’’).
  • An form which must be filled online as the United States Embassy Nairobi Kenya does not accept handwritten forms.
  • Evidence of payment of the fee which can be paid at any Postbank or you can pay it via Mpesa. In case you pay with Mpesa, then you will provide the nine digits that come with the Mpesa message. Also, note that this fee is non-refundable. The fees in KES is 16,800/=.
  • Evidence of your financial status like an original bank statement from any accredited bank recognized by the Central Bank.
  • Any other documentation that the United States embassy might request for that will back up your traveling reasons.

    US Visa Interview Questions in Kenya

    Whenever a person applies for US Visa, the applicant must attend an office interview at the United States consulate or embassy in Gigiri, Nairobi. Interview appointments are booked online and are handed out in a first-come, first-served criterion. The United States Embassy Nairobi conducts typically office interviews every morning from Monday to Thursday.

    Hopefuls without the relevant documentation are not interviewed; hence, you need to double check that you have all forms with you beforehand. Questions asked at United States Embassy in Kenya vary from applicant to applicant as well as the reason for traveling. Usually, the interview will take approximately five to ten minutes after which the interviewer may accept or reject your application or put your case in ‘Administrative Processing.’

    On July 2017, the US Visa appointment was extended from seven days before office interviews to 30 days. This move was not well welcomed by many as it was feared that those with urgent deals abroad were significantly affected. Your application will be processed and decided within 2-3days after your office interview.

    Application passport photo requirements

    The photograph attached to your application should follow some laid guidelines; otherwise, your request might be declined. The following should be taken into account:

    US visa validity

    The validity has nothing to do with the length of time you are allowed in the country. You will only be authorized to remain in the United States by the Department of Homeland Security. If you hold a valid travel document, then you can travel to the nation multiple times for the same purpose you stated.

    If it expires while you are in the country and you have not completed the purpose that made you travel, don’t panic. This is because you can stay as long as the length of stay stated by the Department of Homeland Security on Form I-94 has not come to an end. However, you should depart as soon the official stipulated time comes to an end.

    If you hold a non-immigrant card and you want to renew it before it expires, then you will have to go through the whole process. You will start a new application, go through the interview, and let the responsible officers gauge whether you are qualified to get a new US visa. Nonetheless, sometimes, you might not be interviewed again.

    US visa denials

    Responsible officers may reject your US visa application for some reason. In such a case, your application fee will not be refunded as it covers processing costs. Regardless of whether you received or was denied the document, the amount cannot be reimbursed. Also if it is repudiated and you decide to apply again, you will pay a new processing fee.

    Some refused applications may need to undergo further administrative processing. In such a case, the officers in charge will inform you as soon as you complete your interview. The duration of this exercise might vary depending on your case’s circumstances. Wait for around 180 days unless you have to travel to the United States due to an emergency (illness, injury or death of family member).

    Moving into and out of the United States from Kenya

    A US visa card doesn’t guarantee you an automatic entry into the United States. It only allows you to travel to the country’s port of entries (Airport or land borders). While in there, then you have to request for permission to be allowed in. The Department of Homeland Security has the authority to deny or permit you to the United States.
