The Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) was forced to pull down a perceived insensitive tweet that, according to netizens, awfully described death.

The investigations agency had taken to social media to report an incident where a Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) officer was involved in a domestic dispute with his wife, Violet Asale.

However, a section of Kenyans took issue with how the information was packaged. Some argued the DCI's choice of words was too comical and flowery, despite reporting on a serious matter like death.

"...the wife served him three major bites that promoted him to eternal rest. The 37-year-old officer died in his house when his wife's mighty jaws cast her teeth through his thumb, chest and shoulder," read the DCI 's statement in part.

Following the backlash, the investigation body apologised in a subsequent tweet, vowing not to repeat.

"We have noted with regret that one of our tweets earlier today, did not go down well in as far as our human touch, affection and feelings are concerned. We beg pardon as it was not intended and sincerely commit, that it shall not be repeated again. The Admin of the account has since taken the necessary action," tweeted DCI.

In a separate story, US media had hailed the 29-year-old police constable John Kimani behind the creative DCI tweets.

Kimani's tweets on DCI's Twitter page always inform and entertains as he uses words artistically to paint the exact picture of crime scenes, investigations, and suspects' arrest.

His mastery of the Queen's language earned him international recognition after the Washington Post described his narrations as "Shakespearean".
