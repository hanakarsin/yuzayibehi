Cristiano Ronaldo: Portuguese star named in highest-scoring player of all-time of all positions

August 2024 · 3 minute read

- Cristiano Ronaldo’s goal against Sassuolo has leveled him up with Josef Biscan in the all-time chart

- The Portuguese legend has been named in the all-time scorer in every position

- Ronald Koeman, Frank Lampard are among the legends who have made this unique list

Cristiano Ronaldo has equalled Josef Biscan’s record of the highest goal scorer ever in the game of football as now he is on the verge of breaking the record.

Ronaldo got a late goal in the 3-1 win over Sassuolo on Sunday night, January 11, to take his goals tally to 759, same with what Biscan had during his remarkable career.

The goal did not come that easy as the 35-year-old laboured in the 92nd minute to score Juve's third to put the game beyond the reach of Sassuolo.

GiveMeSport was able to identify the sport's most lethal player in history from every broad position ranging from centre-forwards to goalkeepers and as expected, Cristiano has scored more than any other player in every other position in football.

Below is the list

Goalkeeper - Rogerio Ceni (131 goals)

Centre-back - Ronald Koeman (253 goals)

Right-back - Graham Alexander (107 goals)

Left-back - Paul Breitner (113 goals)

Right/central midfielder - Frank Lampard (274 goals)

Left-midfield - John Wark (223 goals)

Winger - Cristiano Ronaldo (759 goals)

Striker - Josef Bican (759 goals)

Goalkeeper Rogerio Ceni is a name to note as he was known for his examplary set piece abilities despite being between the sticks.

He began taking set-pieces with Sao Paolo in 1997 and went on with his impressive goal scoring form for a keeper till his retirement in 2015.

On the other hand, Lionel Messi has continued his remarkable goal scoring form in his 17th season playing for Barcelona.

The 33-year- old scored a brace during La Blaugrana's 3-2 win over Athletic Bilbao in La Liga making the 17th consecutive year he found the back of the next.
