Breathtaking photos of the new road linking Miritini SGR terminus to Mombasa CBD

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- The road linking the Miritini SGR terminus to Mombasa Island is now fully operational

- Madaraka Express commuters who have used it describe it as an engineering masterpiece

- Commuters previously had problems connecting Mombasa CBD and Miritini due to poor roads

- Construction of the 22-kilometre line linking Miritini terminus to Mombasa CBD station will begin in September, 2018

Commuters using the Madaraka Express trains to and from Mombasa have in recent months pleaded for a proper road linking the Miritini SGR terminus to Mombasa Island and it appears they have received more than they bargained for.

Construction of a road linking the two is almost complete, with pictures of some of its sections revealing a breathtaking engineering masterpiece.

Madaraka Express passengers who have used the road so far are full of praise for the piece of infrastructure whose magnificence they confess has added to their travel experience.

Mohamed Hersi, a veteran hotelier who is now the group operations director with Kenya's leading tour operator, Pollman Tours and Safaris, had a rare review for the long-awaited link road.

“Getting to SGR Miritini is now a very smooth ride. Long term this same road will connect to the Southern bypass to Diani. For those driving from Nairobi, take the interchange just after the Mazeras descent. Such stuff helps me to retain my optimism despite all the challenges we face,” he stated in a social media post, complete with panoramic pictures of the road.“Two years ago, we told some pessimists to watch this space on the coming of SGR and changes associated with it…The space is now a masterpiece,” noted Jaffar Musungu.

His observation was echoed by Sammy M’babe, who averred that Mombasa, especially Changamwe area, has been transformed to an unimaginable extent courtesy of the SGR.

Dipak Galaiya, who traveled from Mombasa to Nairobi using the SGR on Monday,August 20, 2018 with his family, was quick to note that he was impressed with the service, with traffic on the Makupa causeway being the only drawback.

“Drove through that road sometimes last month and its breathtaking, more so when passing through the tunnel. It’s incredibly magnificent. Once the lights are fixed we'll in a different world at night #twasongambele,” added Sam Githinji.

News on the road’s status comes hot in the heels of the announcement that construction of the 22-kilometre line linking the Miritini passenger terminus to Mombasa CBD station will begin at the end of this month.

The expansion of the railway, which is set to be completed in about 12 months, will facilitate faster transit of passengers between Miritini and Mombasa and also enable cargo discharged from ships to be loaded directly onto trains.
