1. Daily Nation

The Kalenjin Myoot Council of Elders has named DP William Ruto the community's spokesperson and endorsed his bid to vie for president in 2022.

The leaders said following consultation with elders from the 12 subtribes, they also decided to endorse move by former Bomet governor Isaac Ruto to join Ruto's camp.

They described Baringo senator Gideon Moi, who is also eying the presidency, as a leader who is yet to mature politically.

According to the leaders, Ruto was equal to the task since he joined politics in the '90s thus, unlike Gideon who rolled up his sleeves after his father Daniel Moi retired in 2002.

The declaration by Myoot elders comes months after Ruto was crowned by the Talai elders.

An attempt by Gideon to meet and be blessed by the elders was recently blocked.

2. The Standard

The ODM party has said it will soon run advertisements calling for people who want to run for president on the party's ticket to tender their bids.

Move by ODM to start focusing on the 2022 presidential election is believed to have been informed by speculations that President Uhuru Kenyatta is likely not to throw his weight behind Raila Odinga in next year's election.

The party also believes it needs to hit the ground running since DP Ruto who is also eying the top seat, has been campaigning.

The Standard also reports that ODM thinks Uhuru's Mount Kenya region may not add so much value in ODM's vote basket in the 2022 presidential poll.

"We have always done well without Central in the past elections. It will not be do or die so long as we get a few votes from the region and retain our strongholds," said ODM deputy party leader Wycliffe Oparanya.

3. People Daily

ANC leader Musalia Mudavadi has joined other leaders in drumming up support for the impeachment of DP Ruto.

Mudavadi said it is immoral for the second in command to always criticise a government he leads in but at the end of the day, enjoy the perks it provides.

He said the impeachment motion was yet to be tabled before the Parliament but hinted this will be done soon.

Mudavadi, while pitching himself as the best candidate for president come 2022, lashed out at the Jubilee administration for failing Kenyans. He cited heavy borrowing which has choked the economy.

4. The Star

A 30-year-old man who recently lost over KSh 320,000 to city fraudsters has blamed "ghosts" for the incident that shook residents of Nairobi on Tuesday, January 19.

Fredric Otieno, who can not explain exactly how he lost the money he had just withdrawn from a bank, says he suspects the cons sprayed something that prompted him to hand them the money.

According to Otieno, he had withdrawn KSh 25,000 from the bank and as he left, he met two women at the entrance of the bank who claimed they could triple his fortunes and he gave them cash.

He then walked back in the bank and withdrew KSh 270,000 which was later taken by a man working with the two women.

The suspects were arrested after a chase by boda boda riders but Otieno believes more suspects are still on the prowl. The money disappeared in the scuffle.

5. Taifa Leo

The ODM party resolved that all future political arrangements with other parties must be done in a well-structured manner to protect the party's interests.

According to the daily, ODM is regretting signing up for the handshake without a proper roadmap which includes signed agreements.

The situation of regret, according to the daily, has been seen after a petition challenging the swearing-in of Nairobi Deputy Governor Anne Kananu was lodged in court. ODM was hoping to be given the benefit of fronting a deputy governor.

It has also emerged Uhuru may not be keen at supporting Raila in 2022 and that the Mount Kenya region is not likely to give ODM many votes come 2022.
