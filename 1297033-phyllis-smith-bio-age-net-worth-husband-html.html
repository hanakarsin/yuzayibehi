Career

Dancing was the young lady's first passion, and she wanted to connect her life with it. She worked as a burlesque dancer in the 1970s, as well as a football cheerleader for the local team. She ended up fracturing her ankle after she slipped on water in her basement. After retiring from dancing, she got a job as a receptionist and made a few attempts to land roles in commercials.

For years, Smith's primary occupation was as a casting director. Eventually, she decided to pursue an acting career, as she felt she had enough experience that she gained from observing actors during casting sessions.

Her breakthrough role was in the sitcom "The Office". Initially, she and casting director Allison Jones were responsible for auditioning people for the show. However, the producers were so fond of Phyllis that they created a whole character and backstory specifically for her.
