Atwoli furious over Kagwe's circular to stop medics from remitting union dues

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- The circular in question was sent by Kagwe on December 22, 2020 directing medics to stop further remittances of union dues

- Atwoli reminded Kagwe that Kenya is a signatory to international labour conventions which advocate for employees being registered in trade unions

- Atwoli is in another protracted battle between the Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT), and the Teachers Service Commission (TSC)

Central Organization of Trade Unions (COTU) Secretary-General Francis Atwoli is fuming over a circular by Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe that directs medics to stop, with immediate effect, further remittances of union dues.

Atwoli threatened that COTU will seek legal redress if the letter is not withdrawn, on top of prevailing upon president Uhuru Kenyatta to sack him from public office over gross violation of workers' rights.

The circular in question was sent by Kagwe on December 22, 2020 to the Public Service Cabinet Secretary Margaret Kobia asking the addressee to implement the decision of a meeting between the Council of Governors and the ministry of Health ministry and the Council of Governors.

The meeting, which had happened a day earlier, resolved that the ministry and county governments would, from that moment on, stop being the agency to remit union dues for medics.

“We know some government ministries have a tendency to disobey court orders. Should this case go to court, the CS will experience the full force of the union. We will ask the President to sack him,” Atwoli told journalists at an address in his offices at the Tom Mboya Labor College.

Atwoli reminded Kagwe that Kenya is a signatory to international labour conventions which advocate for employees being registered in trade unions as well as entering into collective bargaining agreements with their employers.

He added that the money belongs to medics, which means that the government has no temerity to decide on how they will make contributions with it.

“The government cannot say it will not apply and implement the check-off system was accepted and made into law in 1965, stating that every employer will deduct dues and remit them to unions within 14 days,” he added.

Atwoli has often been at loggerheads with both government and private institutions over remittances in the past, with the other most recent case being against COTU, the Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT), and the Teachers Service Commission (TSC).
