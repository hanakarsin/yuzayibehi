Fred Matiang'i: Nationwide Curfew Extended For Further 60 Days

June 2024 · 3 minute read

- The curfew which had been scheduled to end on May 28 has now been extended to July 21

- Social gatherings and movement have also been prohibited during the enforcement of the curfew

- Essential service providers have however been exempted from the directive

Ministry of Interior CS Fred Matiang'i has extended the nationwide dusk to dawn curfew for further 60 days.

In a gazette notice published on May 28, the curfew is slated to end on July 21.

"The order may be cited as the Public Order (State Curfew) Order, No. 3 of 2021. It shall apply to the entire territory of the Republic of Kenya. It shall apply during the hours of darkness between ten o'clock in the evening and four o'clock in the morning with effect from May 28, 2021, and shall remain in effect for a period of sixty days thereof," stated the gazette notice.

The CS also prohibited public gatherings, processions and movement during the enforcement of the curfew.

"There shall be no public gatherings, processions it movement either alone or as a group during the period of the curfew except as shall be permitted, in writing, by a police officer in charge of the police in a county or a police officer in charge of the police in a division/sub-county," it further states.

Essential workers, including medical professionals and health workers, National security officers and National Government administration officers, Licensed pharmaceuticals companies, pharmacies and drug stores, Licensed broadcasters, and media houses. have all been exempted from the directive.

Other spared out of the directive are the workers attached to Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA), Kenya Airports Authority (KAA), and Licensed Civil Aviation Service Providers, Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA), Kenya Ports Authority (KPA) and Licensed Sea and Port Operators.

Others are Kenya Maritime Authority and Licensed Maritime Service Providers, Power production and distribution companies, Kenya Railways Corporation, Kenya Pipeline Company, Water and sanitation service providers, Food and farm produce processors, distributors, dealers, wholesalers, and transporters.

This comes a month after President Uhuru Kenyatta relaxed some of the measures sanctioned in March.

On March 26, the president reviewed the curfew hours which began from 8pm to 4am following a constant upsurge of coronavirus cases.

Movement was also restricted in five counties believed to have been an epicentre with regards to the infections in the country.

During Labour Day on May 1, the directives were revised, with curfew being scheduled between 10pm and 4am.

The restriction of movement effected in Kajiado, Nairobi, Kiambu, Machakos and Nakuru was also lifted.

The current positivity rate stands at less than 10%.
