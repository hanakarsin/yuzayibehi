Ex-senator Boni Khalwale praises Aisha Jumwa for chasing ODM's Sifuna off podium during burial

May 2024 · 2 minute read

- Malindi MP Aisha Jumwa was the master of ceremonies at the burial of Mombasa deputy governor's father

- The MP invited Sifuna to address mourners and deliver Raila's condolence message

- The ODM official began talking about party loyalty and how they intend to remain strong

- Sifuna's speech irked Aisha who was expelled from the Orange party for failing to toe the line

Former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale is over the moon quite literally after Malindi MP Aisha Jumwa snatched a microphone from ODM Secretary General Edwin Sifuna at a public function.

Khalwale who had no nice words for Sifuna, hailed Aisha for what he termed as commitment to the new Kenya for blocking the latter from addressing mourners at the burial of Mombasa Deputy Governor William Kingi’s father.

In a Facebook rant on Saturday, May 4, the politician did not only ridicule Sifuna but also accused him of downgrading the Orange party by engaging in small political fights.

"Honestly, who is going to advice this greenhorn by the name of Edwin Sifuna? We are making the ODM party play ground so tiny that their jokers will have a very limited area where to fool Kenyans with tibim, tialala, etc. Congratulations Aisha Jumwa for your commitment to the new Kenya," he posted.

At the burial, mourners who included Kilifi Governor Amason Kingi and Siaya Senator James Orengo were treated to drama when Sifuna began to speak matters concerning party loyalty.

Considering she was kicked out of the Opposition party for failing to toe the line, the speech was not going to be a comfortable one for Aisha.

At this point, the visibly furious Malindi politician rose on her feet and started yelling at Sifuna while asking him to stop politicking but instead condole with the bereaved.

Not even boos and shouts from mourners could restore sanity in the intense atmosphere as the former Malindi woman representative stood her ground, charged towards the podium and grabbed the microphone leaving Sifuna staring blankly at the audience.
