Benjamin Zulu asks men to take leadership in their own homes: &quot;Marriage sio nusu mkate&quot;

March 2024 · 4 minute read

- Relationship coach Benjamin Zulu has advised men to have authority in their matrimonial homes and offer leadership

- He said that marriages are not 'nusu-mkate' governments because men should take up more share of responsibilities if they want to be respected

- Zulu urged people to marry partners of their class so that they can develop at the same pace and without disputes or competition

Equity in marriage has been a debatable issue, with people having contradicting opinions. Some believe partners should contribute equally while others believe men should take up bigger responsibilities.

But, relationship coach Benjamin Zulu has a different opinion, and he is now asking men to level up and take up the right spaces in their own matrimonial homes.

While appearing on a talk show on Switch TV the famous life coach explained why there can never be equality in marriage.

''When it comes to marriage, please marry reality; potential is just a dream that may never come to pass. It takes a lot of effort to let potential come to pass, and not everybody is willing to do the work.

You need to establish how you share things in your marital status; I keep telling men if you and your wife pay for things equally, then you will lose your authority as a leader. It may look democratic, but you will lose any say to tell her anything,'' he said.

The psychologist alluded that having a 50-50 input in any relationship can make a woman resent a man because women naturally want leaders in their homes.

''I tell men to take a higher share of responsibilities and when a woman has to take care of the baby, take care of everything else. Show leadership as a man, it will give you authority as a man, you want to have the final say of the direction of your life.In case of a dispute, you want to have the veto power but you earn that by taking more hare of everything in the house. This is not a 'nusu-mkate' government or coalition but partnerships but with a person who is a leader,'' he urged.

Zulu urged people to marry partners of their class so that they can develop at the same pace and without disputes or competition.
