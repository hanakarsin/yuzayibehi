William Ruto silences critics, queues to register for Huduma Namba

June 2024 Â· 2 minute read

- Huduma Namba registration exercise was officially launched by President Uhuru Kenyatta in Machakos on Tuesday, April 2

- Deputy President William Ruto was expected to launch the exercise in Kakamega but failed to show up

- This raised concerns with some observers claiming he was opposed to the process

- His registration at Langata DCC offices arguably brought to an end speculation he was opposed to the exercise

Deputy President William Ruto has registered for Huduma Namba after speculation ensued that the the country's second in command was opposed to the exercise.

Ruto raised questions after skipping the launch of the crucial national function in Kakamega county where he was expected to preside over it.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt the DP accompanied by his son enlisted for Huduma Namba on Tuesday, April 9, at Langata DCC offices and brought to an end speculation that he was against the national exercise being spearheaded by President Uhuru Kenyatta.

The enlisting process, which involved Opposition leaders, was officially launched by Uhuru in Machakos on Tuesday, April 2.

Opposition chief Raila Odinga presided over the process in Mombasa while Kalonzo Musyoka and his FORD Kenya counterpart Moses Wetangu'la were in Murang'a county for a similar task.

Some observers claimed the DP boycotted the launch to protest the incorporation of the Opposition team in what he believed was purely a government function.

In his defence, Ruto said he could not attended the function because he was hosting a Cuban delegation led by the country's Vice President Ines Maria Chapman.
