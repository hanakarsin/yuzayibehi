The Atheists In Kenya Society (AIK) has celebrated the ouster of Ezekiel Mutua from the helm of the Kenya Film Classification Board (KFCB).

In a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke, AIK accused Mutua of making free expression through film and television markedly difficult during his tenure in office.

''Mutua enacted steep license fees that reduced the film industry’s ability to operate independently, including the requirement of filmmakers needing multiple licenses to film in multiple counties,'' the statement reads.

Kenya-based films shot in South Africa

Through its president, Nyende Mumia Nyende, the society claimed that it became common for Kenya-based films and content to be filmed in South Africa during his tenure.

''The self-proclaimed moral police, who became known as deputy Jesus on social media also attempted to enforce his dictates on theatre, the film industry and public life,'' Nyende said.

At the same time, he took a jibe at Mutua on what he termed as unconstitutionally favouring the Christian faith in the running of KFCB.

Mutua's ouster was long overdue

Nyende said that Mutua's ouster from the office was long overdue.

''Article 8 of the Kenyan Constitution prohibits the government (or its officials) from making any law respecting an establishment of religion. Therefore, he AtMutua acted unconstitutionally by unduly favouring the Christian faith in the running of KFCB,'' Nyende explained.

Nyende hoped incoming KCFB leadership would be dynamic, progressive, inclusive and accommodative.

On Saturday, August 7, the former KFCB boss shared a long post on Facebook detailing his accomplishments at the film board and what he hopes the country takes from his fight for clean content.

"Yesterday, all SAGAs under the Ministry of ICT signed the 2021/22 PC vetting with CS Joe Mucheru as required by law. I led the KFCB team of directors and senior managers. From the presentation, KFCB has overperformed in all its targets for the FY 2020/21, in some instances scoring over 300%,'' he said.

Spearheading clean content

He further explained that he was at the forefront calling out the unpalatable content creators and promoting clean content.

Earlier, Mutua had posted online rubbishing rumours that were doing rounds claiming that he had been fired.

He stated that he was still the film boss and that there was no CEO vacancy on the board.
